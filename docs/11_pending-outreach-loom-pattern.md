# Pending Outreach Loom Pattern

Updated: 2026-06-25

## Purpose

Use this when a warm lead might need workflow cleanup, video/content systems, or both.

Marketing-data read:

```text
People expect portfolio.
Personal portfolio beats generic capability.
Concrete "show me how this works" beats vague trust-building.
```

Rule:

```text
Default: send a short message first. If they bite, send a 45-90 second Loom that shows the mechanism.
Exception: send message + video together only for warm referrals, Upwork/proposal contexts, or a very high-signal lead where portfolio is expected.
```

Do not start with a long pitch video. The video is portfolio after curiosity exists.

## Default LinkedIn Opener

```text
Hey [Name], quick question: are you still handling video editing / repurposing manually, or do you already have a clean process for getting clips out?
```

Use this as the first-message opener when the lead has visible video,
repurposing, course, training, social, webinar, podcast, or content operations
signals. If they bite, then offer the audit, cleanup pass, or short Loom.

## Old Video Mold Teardown

The old Upwork/course-edit script had useful portfolio, but the shape was too video-service-first.

Keep:

```text
1,200+ recruiting edits
300+ lessons/modules
raw clips -> publish-ready output
messy folders -> ordered files
AI helps only where it saves time
```

Cut:

```text
generic editor intro
long list of editing services
timeline-heavy portfolio with no buyer-specific gap
"I can do video" as the main claim
```

Translate:

```text
video editing portfolio -> evidence that messy production work can be organized
course edits -> evidence that long content can become repeatable modules
sports reels -> evidence that raw assets can become decision-ready outputs
AI mention -> small practical helper, not the headline
```

Current offer mold:

```text
I have been working with practical AI and messy workflows.
I am offering a free audit to see where I can build something useful for you or your team.
If there is a fit, I can show a small example path.
```

## Two-Sentence Message

Default:

```text
I’ve been helping turn messy workflows into cleaner systems, including small AI-assisted steps where they actually save time. I’m offering a free audit to see if there’s anything useful I could build for you or your team.
```

Warmer:

```text
I’ve been working on practical AI/workflow cleanup for teams with scattered notes, files, content, follow-ups, or repeat tasks. If it’s useful, I’d be happy to do a free audit and see where a simple system could help.
```

Direct:

```text
Would a free AI/workflow audit be useful? I can take a look at one messy process and suggest the first simple thing I’d build or clean up.
```

Personal researched:

```text
I saw you have a few moving pieces across your services/content, and it made me think there might be a practical AI or workflow cleanup opportunity there. If you’re open to it, I can do a free audit and send back the first simple system I’d build.
```

Most casual:

```text
I’ve been building practical AI/workflow systems for messy notes, files, content, and follow-ups. If you’re open to it, I’d be happy to do a free audit and see if there’s anything useful I could build for you.
```

## If They Say Yes

Send a Loom that proves one of these:

```text
workflow portfolio: before -> cleaner handoff
video portfolio: raw content -> reusable production path
AI portfolio: messy input -> structured output
marketing portfolio: content/workflow portfolio -> buyer-facing page or campaign
```

Keep the video focused on one mechanism. Do not explain the whole business.

## 45-Second Loom

```text
0:00-0:05
Quick context:
"I noticed one place where the workflow/content path looks like it could be tightened."

0:05-0:20
Show the current state:
page, folder, content path, service path, intake, handoff, or portfolio gap.

0:20-0:35
Show the cleaner version:
simple folder map, checklist, content path, review path, or example hub.

0:35-0:45
Close:
"This is the kind of cleanup I help with: make the path easier to see, repeat, and finish. If useful, I can map the first version with you."
```

## 90-Second Loom

```text
0:00-0:08
Name the specific thing noticed.

0:08-0:25
Show the messy or unclear path.

0:25-0:45
Show the likely bottleneck:
intake, naming, content order, approvals, handoff, follow-up, publishing, or portfolio.

0:45-1:10
Show the cleaner mechanism:
workflow hub, file structure, repeatable checklist, content repurpose map, or AI-assisted step.

1:10-1:25
Show why it matters:
less guessing, easier review, smoother publishing, clearer handoff, reusable process.

1:25-1:30
Ask:
"Want me to map the first clean version?"
```

## Boilerplate Demo Script

```text
Hey, I’m Jerami. I’ve been working on practical AI and workflow cleanup: the kind of stuff that helps organize messy notes, files, content, follow-ups, and repeated tasks.

The point is not to throw AI at everything. It is to look at one messy process and see where a small system, prompt, checklist, folder path, or review flow could save time.

For a free audit, I’d look at where the work starts, where the files or notes live, what gets reviewed, what gets sent or published, and where things usually slow down.

Then I’d suggest the first simple thing I’d build. That might be a workflow hub, cleaner folders, a repeatable content checklist, or one AI-assisted step that turns messy input into something usable.

The goal is just to make the work easier to see, easier to repeat, and easier to finish.
```

## Researched Lead Loom Script

Use this when you already found a real gap.

```text
Hey, I’m Jerami. I took a quick look at what you’re building, and I think there may be a practical AI or workflow cleanup opportunity here.

This is not a full teardown or me pretending I know your whole business. I just want to show the kind of thing I’d look for in a free audit.

Usually I’m looking for where notes, files, content, follow-ups, reviews, or repeated tasks start to get scattered.

The first thing I’d map is simple: where the work starts, where the assets live, what needs review, what gets sent or published, and how the next step is tracked.

From there, I’d look for one useful build: a small workflow hub, cleaner folder path, content checklist, or lightweight AI-assisted step for summaries, sorting, review prep, or follow-up.

The reason I’m showing it this way is because AI is only useful if it fits the real workflow.

If useful, I can do the free audit and send back the first version I’d build.
```

## Portfolio Insert Options

Use one portfolio insert max.

Workflow portfolio:

```text
I have built systems where scattered notes, tasks, files, and follow-ups get turned into one reusable working path.
```

Video portfolio:

```text
I have also handled high-volume video work: recruiting edits, course lessons, long recordings, and publish-ready libraries.
```

AI portfolio:

```text
When AI helps, I use it to structure messy inputs, summarize, route, or prep review. I do not make it the whole offer.
```

Combined portfolio:

```text
The useful overlap is workflow plus content: organize the work behind the video, then the actual content gets easier to create, review, and ship.
```

## When To Use Which Angle

```text
Pure workflow:
Use when the lead has multiple services, sister businesses, team handoffs, intake, follow-up, or unclear operating paths.

Video/content:
Use when the lead has educational content, social posts, webinars, course material, media pages, or underused video.

AI assist:
Use only when there is a clear repeated task: summaries, structure, content sorting, transcript cleanup, task extraction, or review prep.

Marketing:
Use only when workflow/content portfolio already connects to a page, offer, audience path, or campaign.
```

## What Not To Say

Avoid:

```text
I can run ads for you.
You need a full funnel.
Your brand is doing this wrong.
I built an AI system.
I can fix everything.
```

Use:

```text
I noticed one fixable path.
Here is the current gap.
Here is the cleaner mechanism.
This is the first version I would map.
```
