# Video Editing Tutor Loom Notes

## Core thesis

I genuinely like learning how someone thinks, then building the editing system around them.

## Card 1: Open

You want to learn Premiere by actually making your own videos.

That is exactly the kind of call I enjoy: sit together, solve the thing in front of us, and make the next edit easier.

## Card 2: Premiere

Show: bins + timeline.

Color-coded bins. Clear names. Markers. Organized tracks.

Quick flex: Quiver / Excalibur.

Shortcuts: `Z`, `shift+Z`, `A/S`.

Say: “I like finding the small changes that make editing feel natural and fast.”

## Card 3: Short-form

Hooks. Dead space. Pacing. Captions. Templates.

Pick 2-3 creators you love.

Study what works, then build your version of it.

## Card 4: Teaching

Show: NurseHub / course / structured lesson.

Say: “A lot of my work has been making complicated material clear and easy to follow. That carries directly into teaching an edit.”

## Card 5: My actual fit

Most of my work has been long-form and brand-guided.

That taught me to listen closely, follow a style, and notice the details that matter to one specific client.

I am honestly excited by the chance to learn your taste and build around it.

## Card 6: Workflow + AI

Show: folders, templates, naming, Eagle, or the Premiere project.

Name things early. Touch footage once. Make repeat work easier.

We can build a simple shared hub for requests, references, notes, and reviews.

AI can help where it is useful: sorting, rough organization, captions, or repetitive cleanup.

## Close

First, we make you comfortable editing.

Then, we turn what works into templates and a workflow.

Over time, I can learn the system well enough to take on more of the editing.

Keep this in your head:

“I want to learn how you think, help you edit with confidence, and build the system around your style.”
